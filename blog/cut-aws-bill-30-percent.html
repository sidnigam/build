How to Cut Your AWS Bill by 30% in 30 Days

By Sid Nigam · January 15, 2026 · 6 min read

Cloud spend creep is one of the most common problems we see at Nimbus Tech Solutions. Within 60 days of going live, most SMBs are paying 30–50% more than they need to. The good news: the fixes are straightforward and don't require re-architecting your stack.

1. Right-Size Your EC2 Instances

AWS Compute Optimizer analyzes your CloudWatch metrics and flags instances running at under 40% CPU. We regularly see companies running m5.2xlarge instances doing the work of a t3.medium. Right-sizing alone typically yields 15–20% savings.

2. Purchase Savings Plans

If you've been running on-demand for more than 3 months, your usage pattern is predictable enough for Compute Savings Plans. A 1-year no-upfront commitment saves ~30% vs. on-demand. Start with covering your baseline steady-state usage.

3. Migrate Batch Workloads to Spot Instances

Any workload that can tolerate interruption — data processing, CI/CD runners, ML training — should run on Spot. Combined with Karpenter for Kubernetes or EC2 Auto Scaling, you can save 70–90% vs. on-demand on eligible workloads.

4. Hunt Down Idle Resources

Run AWS Trusted Advisor or the open-source cloud-nuke tool to find: unattached EBS volumes, idle load balancers, unused Elastic IPs, and forgotten RDS instances in non-prod environments. We find $500–$2,000/month in idle resources in almost every audit.

5. Set S3 Lifecycle Policies

Move objects not accessed in 30 days to S3-IA, and objects not accessed in 90 days to Glacier Instant Retrieval. For log buckets, set a 365-day expiry. This is often a $200–$800/month saving for companies with large S3 footprints.
